Project details

In summation, the examination of both Zimmer's case study on Facebook data research and Buchanan's case study on the ethics of big data research emphasizes the significance of enacting privacy legislation akin to Europe's new Privacy legislation in the United States. The use of ethical methods, specifically contractarianism, provides a framework for evaluating the measures done in these cases and advocating for the passage of privacy laws that value informed consent, privacy, and equity. The research demonstrates that the behaviors adopted in both cases violated fundamental ethical norms. In the instance of Facebook data research, violations of informed permission and data contextual integrity jeopardized people's rights to control their data and the context in which it is used. Similarly, ethical issues were raised about big data research on Twitter due to a lack of informed permission, privacy protection, and the potential harm to individuals. According to this judgment, the proper thing to do would have been to respect persons' rights and follow ethical principles throughout the study process
